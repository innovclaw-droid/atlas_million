## Lesson 1: AI Email Templates That Convert

### Script

Email is still the highest-ROI communication channel in real estate. But most agents either send generic templates or spend too long personalizing each one. AI gives you the best of both — personalized emails at template speed.

**The 5 Essential Email Templates Every Agent Needs:**

**1. New Lead Response (speed-to-lead is everything):**

> "Write a response email for a new buyer lead who submitted an inquiry on [property/website]. Respond within the context of what they asked about. Include: acknowledgment of their interest, one relevant piece of value (market insight or property detail), and a clear next step (phone call or showing). Keep under 100 words. Tone: responsive, helpful, not salesy."

**2. Listing Presentation Follow-Up:**

> "Write a follow-up email after meeting with a potential seller. Reference [specific thing discussed]. Attach the CMA (mention it). Reaffirm why you're the right agent. Include a clear CTA to sign the listing agreement. Under 150 words."

**3. Under Contract Update:**

> "Write a transaction update email to [buyer/seller] client. We are at [stage: inspection/appraisal/clear to close]. Explain what this stage means, what happens next, and any action items they need to complete. Tone: reassuring, organized."

**4. Sphere Touch (Stay Top of Mind):**

> "Write a casual check-in email to a past client I haven't spoken to in 3-4 months. Don't ask for referrals directly. Include a relevant market update or helpful tip. Make it feel like a friend checking in, not an agent fishing for business. Under 100 words."

**5. Price Reduction Conversation:**

> "Write an email to a seller suggesting a price reduction. Current price: $[X]. Suggested new price: $[X]. We've been on market [X] days with [X] showings. Tone: honest but empathetic. Present data to support the recommendation. Don't blame the seller."

Each template saves 10-15 minutes. If you send 10 emails a day, that's 1-2 hours saved daily.

### Action Items
1. Generate all 5 email templates customized for your business
2. Save them in a Google Doc or your CRM's template library
3. Use at least 2 this week and note the response
4. Track: which AI emails get better responses than your old ones?

## Lesson 2: Follow-Up Sequences on Autopilot

### Script

The money is in the follow-up. 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups, but most agents stop after 1-2. AI writes the entire sequence — you just schedule and send.

**The Buyer Lead Nurture (7-Email Sequence):**

> "Write a 7-email nurture sequence for a buyer lead who inquired about homes in [area] but hasn't scheduled a showing yet. Space emails 3-5 days apart.
>
> Email 1: Quick intro + 1 helpful tip about buying in [area]
> Email 2: New listing alert — describe a hypothetical ideal property for them
> Email 3: Educational — 'What most buyers don't know about [local market detail]'
> Email 4: Social proof — mention helping a similar buyer recently (generic, no names)
> Email 5: Value add — link to your buyer's guide or market report
> Email 6: Personal touch — share something about the neighborhood (restaurant, park, event)
> Email 7: Final check-in — low pressure, 'still looking? Happy to help when you're ready'
>
> Each email under 100 words. Tone: patient, helpful, human. Never pushy."

**The Seller Long-Term Nurture (Monthly for 6 months):**

> "Write 6 monthly emails for a homeowner who said they might sell 'in a year or so.' Each email should provide genuine value without pushing to list.
>
> Month 1: Market update for their neighborhood
> Month 2: Home improvement tips that increase value
> Month 3: Interest rate impact on buyer demand
> Month 4: Seasonal selling advantages
> Month 5: What the selling process actually looks like (demystify)
> Month 6: Soft check-in — 'thinking about timing?'
>
> Under 120 words each. Tone: trusted advisor."

**The Re-Engagement Sequence (Cold Leads):**

> "Write a 3-email re-engagement sequence for leads who went cold 3+ months ago. Don't acknowledge that they ghosted you. Just provide fresh value and a new reason to respond. Under 80 words each."

### Action Items
1. Build your buyer nurture sequence (7 emails)
2. Build your seller long-term sequence (6 emails)
3. Build one re-engagement sequence for cold leads
4. Load them into your CRM or email tool
5. Assign them to at least 10 leads this week

## Lesson 3: Handling Objections with AI Scripts

### Script

Every agent faces the same objections. AI helps you prepare perfect responses so you're never caught off guard.

**The Top 10 Objections + AI Responses:**

For each, use this prompt:

> "Write a professional, empathetic response to a real estate client who says: '[objection]'. Keep it conversational — this is spoken, not written. Under 100 words. Don't be defensive. Acknowledge their concern, then redirect with value."

**The objections:**

1. "Your commission is too high"
2. "I can sell it myself (FSBO)"
3. "My neighbor's agent will do it for less"
4. "I want to wait for the market to go up"
5. "Zillow says my home is worth more"
6. "I need to think about it"
7. "Can you reduce your commission?"
8. "We want to interview other agents"
9. "Why should I list now instead of spring?"
10. "The house down the street sold for more — I want that price"

Generate responses for all 10 and practice them out loud. Having a prepared, thoughtful response to every common objection is what separates top producers from average agents.

**The Role-Play Prompt:**

> "Let's role-play. You're a skeptical homeowner and I'm the listing agent. You'll present objections and I'll practice responding. Start with: 'I think your commission is too high. Can you do 4% instead of 5%?'"

AI will play the difficult client. Practice until your responses feel natural.

### Action Items
1. Generate responses to all 10 objections above
2. Add any objections unique to your market
3. Print them out or save them on your phone for quick reference
4. Role-play with AI for 15 minutes — it's free practice
5. Use one prepared response this week in a real conversation

## Lesson 4: Client Update Templates

### Script

Keeping clients informed during a transaction builds trust and prevents the "Why haven't I heard from you?" call. AI creates update templates for every stage.

**Transaction Stage Updates:**

> "Write brief client update emails for each stage of a real estate transaction. Buyer side. Each under 80 words. Reassuring tone.
>
> 1. Offer submitted — waiting for response
> 2. Offer accepted — next steps
> 3. Inspection scheduled
> 4. Inspection complete — results summary
> 5. Appraisal ordered
> 6. Appraisal received — on track
> 7. Loan cleared to close
> 8. Closing day prep — what to bring
> 9. Post-closing — congratulations + next steps
>
> For each: explain what happened, what's next, and any action items for the client."

That's your entire transaction communication plan. Load these into your CRM and trigger them at each stage. Your clients feel informed at every step.

**The Weekly Check-In:**

> "Write a weekly check-in template for active buyer clients who are in the house search phase. Short, casual. Include: any new listings that match their criteria, a market insight, and encouragement. Under 60 words."

### Action Items
1. Generate all 9 transaction stage emails for buyer side
2. Generate the seller-side equivalents (listing live, showing feedback, offer received, etc.)
3. Create the weekly check-in template
4. Load all templates into your CRM
5. Use them on your next active transaction

## Lesson 5: Review & Referral Requests

### Script

Reviews and referrals are the highest-converting lead source — but most agents feel awkward asking. AI writes the ask so it feels natural, not desperate.

**The Review Request (Post-Closing):**

> "Write an email asking a client to leave a Google/Zillow review after closing. Wait 1 week after closing. Reference a specific positive moment from the transaction (e.g., 'I loved helping you find the perfect place for your family'). Make it easy — include a direct link. Express genuine gratitude. Don't make it sound like a template. Under 100 words."

**The Referral Request (60-90 Days Post-Close):**

> "Write a casual email to a past client (60-90 days post-close) that naturally leads to a referral ask. Start with a genuine check-in about how they're enjoying their new home. Then: 'If you know anyone thinking about buying or selling, I'd love to help them the same way.' Keep it warm and brief. Under 80 words."

**The Annual Touch:**

> "Write a home anniversary email for a client who bought their home exactly 1 year ago today. Celebrate the milestone. Include: approximate equity gained based on [X%] appreciation, a tip for home maintenance, and a soft referral ask. Warm, celebratory tone. Under 120 words."

**The Testimonial Prompt:**

Sometimes clients want to leave a review but don't know what to say:

> "My client wants to leave me a review but asked me to draft something they can edit. Write a genuine-sounding testimonial from the perspective of [buyer/seller]. Mention: [1-2 specific things I helped with]. Keep it authentic — not over-the-top. 3-4 sentences."

Send it saying "Here's a draft if it helps — feel free to change anything!" Most clients use it as-is.

### Action Items
1. Generate your review request email
2. Generate your referral request email
3. Generate the annual home anniversary email
4. Create a testimonial draft for your most recent closing
5. Send at least one review request and one referral request this week
