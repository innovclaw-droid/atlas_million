## Lesson 1: AI Content Calendar Generator

### Script

Consistency beats perfection on social media. The agents who post daily get more leads than the ones who post a masterpiece once a month. AI makes daily posting effortless.

**The 30-Day Calendar Prompt:**

> "Create a 30-day social media content calendar for a real estate agent in [city]. I post on Instagram and Facebook.
>
> Content mix:
> - 40% Educational (market tips, buying/selling advice, AI tools)
> - 25% Listings & Market (properties, market updates, just sold)
> - 20% Personal/Behind-the-scenes (day in the life, client wins, local spots)
> - 15% Engagement (questions, polls, this-or-that)
>
> For each day provide:
> - Post type (carousel, reel, story, static)
> - Topic/headline
> - Caption (under 150 words)
> - 5 relevant hashtags
> - Best time to post
>
> Make it specific to [city] real estate."

One prompt. 30 days of content. Review, customize with your photos, schedule.

**Monthly Theming:**

Each month, anchor your content around a theme:

- January: "New Year, New Home" + market predictions
- March/April: "Spring Market is Here" + prep tips
- June: "Summer Moving Season" + neighborhood guides
- September: "Back to School" + family neighborhoods
- November: "Holiday Selling Tips" + gratitude posts

> "Adjust my 30-day calendar for [month]. Theme: [theme]. Include seasonal hooks and timely content."

### Action Items
1. Generate your first 30-day content calendar
2. Identify which posts you can batch-create this weekend
3. Schedule the first week using your favorite scheduling tool
4. Track engagement — which post types get the most interaction?

## Lesson 2: Instagram & Facebook Post Formulas

### Script

Every great social post follows a formula. Here are 5 that work consistently for real estate agents — and AI writes all of them.

**Formula 1: The Tip Post**

> "Write an Instagram caption sharing one practical tip for [first-time buyers/sellers/investors] in [city]. Structure: Hook question → Tip → Why it matters → CTA to save the post. Under 100 words. Include 5 hashtags."

**Formula 2: The Myth Buster**

> "Write a 'myth vs reality' Instagram caption about real estate. Myth: [common misconception]. Reality: [the truth]. Make it punchy and shareable. End with 'Save this for when someone tells you [myth].' Under 80 words."

**Formula 3: The Before/After**

> "Write a caption for a before/after post. Before: [agent doing task manually]. After: [agent using AI to do it in seconds]. Make it relatable and slightly humorous. End with a question to drive comments. Under 80 words."

**Formula 4: The Market Update**

> "Write a market update caption for [city] real estate this month. Data: [2-3 key stats]. Make it digestible for non-experts. Include what it means for buyers AND sellers. Under 100 words."

**Formula 5: The Personal Story**

> "Write a personal/behind-the-scenes caption about [scenario: just helped a first-time buyer close, early morning showing, tough negotiation win]. Make it authentic and relatable. Show personality. End with a lesson or insight. Under 120 words."

### Action Items
1. Write one post using each of the 5 formulas
2. Schedule them across this week (1 per day)
3. Note which formula gets the best engagement
4. Build a rotation — cycle through formulas so content never feels repetitive

## Lesson 3: Video Scripts & Reel Ideas

### Script

Video is king. Reels get 2-3x more reach than static posts. But most agents freeze when the camera turns on. AI writes the script — you just deliver it.

**The 30-Second Reel Script:**

> "Write a 30-second Instagram Reel script for a real estate agent. Topic: [e.g., '3 things to know before buying in Austin']. Format:
> - Hook (first 2 seconds — must stop the scroll)
> - 3 quick points (5-8 seconds each)
> - CTA (follow for more tips)
>
> Write it as spoken words — casual, energetic, like talking to a friend. Include suggested text overlays for each scene."

**The Walkthrough Script:**

> "Write a script for a 60-second property walkthrough Reel. Property: [key details]. Don't just list features — tell a story. 'Imagine coming home to...' Use sensory language. End with price and CTA."

**Batch Your Reels:**

Film day: set aside 2 hours, film 8-10 reels using AI scripts. That's 2+ weeks of content from one session.

> "Give me 10 Reel ideas for a real estate agent in [city]. Mix: 4 educational tips, 3 property-related, 2 personal/funny, 1 trending. For each, write the hook (first line) and a 3-sentence outline."

### Action Items
1. Generate 10 Reel ideas with hooks
2. Write full scripts for your top 3
3. Film at least 1 this week — just use your phone
4. Schedule a monthly "film day" to batch content

## Lesson 4: LinkedIn for Real Estate Pros

### Script

LinkedIn is underused by real estate agents — and that's your opportunity. The algorithm favors text posts, the audience is professional (think: relocating executives, investors, first-time buyers with good jobs), and there's less competition.

**LinkedIn Post Formula:**

> "Write a LinkedIn post for a real estate agent. Topic: [market insight / career lesson / client success story]. Structure:
> - Opening hook (1 line, bold statement or question)
> - Story or insight (4-6 lines, with line breaks for readability)
> - Key takeaway (1-2 lines)
> - Question to drive comments
>
> LinkedIn audience: professionals, investors, relocating executives. Tone: professional but human. No hashtags in the body — add 3 at the very end."

**Content That Works on LinkedIn:**

- Market insights with data ("Austin home prices rose 8% this year. Here's what that means...")
- Lessons from your career ("10 years in real estate taught me 3 things...")
- Client success stories (anonymized, with permission)
- Industry commentary ("Why I think the housing market in 2025...")
- Relocation content (great for attracting out-of-state leads)

**The LinkedIn Connection Strategy:**

> "Write a LinkedIn connection request message for [a local business owner / someone who just posted about relocating / a potential investor]. Keep it under 50 words. Personal, not generic. Don't pitch — just connect."

### Action Items
1. Write 3 LinkedIn posts using the formula above
2. Post one this week
3. Send 5 connection requests to potential clients in your area
4. Commit to 2 LinkedIn posts per week for the next month

## Lesson 5: Batch Content Creation Workflow

### Script

Let's put everything together. Here's the system for creating an entire month of social media content in one sitting.

**The Monthly Batch Workflow (2-3 Hours Total):**

**Hour 1: Generate (AI does the heavy lifting)**
1. Generate 30-day content calendar (10 min)
2. Write all captions — Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn (30 min)
3. Generate 8-10 Reel scripts (15 min)
4. Create 2-3 carousel outlines (10 min)

**Hour 2: Create (You add the visuals)**
1. Create graphics in Canva using AI-generated copy (30 min)
2. Film Reels using AI scripts (30 min)
3. Screenshot market data for market update posts (10 min)

**Hour 3: Schedule (Automate delivery)**
1. Upload everything to your scheduling tool (Later, Buffer, Hootsuite, Meta Business Suite)
2. Set optimal posting times
3. Prepare Stories content for the first 2 weeks

**That's it. One afternoon. 30 days of consistent, professional content.**

**The Master Prompt (All-in-One):**

> "Create a complete social media content package for a real estate agent in [city] for [month]:
>
> 1. 30 Instagram/Facebook post captions (mix of educational, listings, personal, engagement)
> 2. 8 Reel scripts (30-second format with hooks and text overlays)
> 3. 4 LinkedIn posts (professional, insight-driven)
> 4. 4 Story ideas (polls, Q&As, behind-the-scenes)
> 5. Monthly email newsletter draft (market update + 1 tip + CTA)
>
> All content specific to [city] real estate. Include hashtags where appropriate."

### Action Items
1. Block 3 hours on your calendar this weekend for batch creation
2. Run the Master Prompt and review the output
3. Create visuals in Canva for the first 2 weeks
4. Schedule everything
5. Repeat monthly — it gets faster each time
